Teriyaki Steak Recipe

This beef teriyaki is a homemade version of one of my favorite restaurant dishes. Every piece of beef is a little crispy and coated in a sweet and sticky teriyaki sauce! It’s delicious served over steamed rice for a quick weeknight dinner.

Ingedients:

    For the beef
  • 2 lbs flank steak
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 4-6 tablespoons coconut oil, or cooking oil of choice

    • For the sauce
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce, use tamari for gluten-free
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon corn-starch
  • 1/4 teaspon red pepper flakes
  • 3 green onions, white and green parts thinly sliced
  • 3 gloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated

Steps:

  1. Slice the flank against the grain into strips, as thin as possible. Place the steak in a ziploc bag and add the cornstarch. Toss to evenly coat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the tamari, brown sugar, water, cornstarch and red pepper flakes. Stir to dissolve the sugar and cornstarch.
  3. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add 2-3 tablespoons coconut oil and heat until hot. Shake off any excess cornstarch and carefully add ½ the beef in an even layer, breaking up any chunks. Let cook undisturbed for 4-5 minutes. Using tongs, flip the beef to the other side and let cook for another 2-3 minutes. Transfer to a plate. Repeat with the remaining oil and beef.
  4. After transferring the second batch of cooked beef to a plate, Add a little more oil to the pan if needed and turn the heat to medium. Add the green onions, garlic and ginger and cook being careful not to burn for about 1-2 minutes. Add the sauce mixture and stir to combine. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let cook for 4-5 minutes until it thickens. Add the beef and serve immediately so the beef stays crispy. Serve with rice. Enjoy!
